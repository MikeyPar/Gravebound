## Required behavior

- Represent `fan`, `ring_with_gap`, `telegraphed_lane`, and `fixed_timeline` as immutable, renderer-independent definitions. Existing enemy and Bell Proctor attack primitives must adapt without changing their scheduler-owned values.
- Require stable lowercase content IDs. `telegraph_id` is exactly `pattern_id + ".telegraph"`; `audio_cue_id` is exactly `pattern_id + ".warning"`. Major, Severe, and Execution definitions also require the exact `.warning.major` cue.
- Store and validate origin cue, shape cue, combat color family, raw damage, `DamageType`, `DamageBand`, first/repeated warnings, lifetime/active duration, projectile speed/radius when applicable, counterplay, memory family, disposition, threat, maximum active instances, compatibility tags, statuses, pierce policy, acceleration, and phase-cancel policy.
- Compile hostile warning milliseconds with ceiling-to-tick. Enforce exact GDD minimums: Chip `250/200 ms`, Pressure `400/300`, Major `650/500`, Severe `900/750`, Execution `1200/1000`. Repeated warning may be used only after the complete mechanic has been shown and cannot exceed the authored first warning.
- Fan definitions require a positive projectile count and exactly that many strictly ordered offsets. Their shape/counterplay/disposition grammar is Fan/Strafe/ConsumeOnPlayerOrSolid.
- Ring-with-gap requires at least three indices, a nonzero proper omitted run, and a nonzero advance below the index count. Its grammar is RingGap/FollowGap/ConsumeOnPlayerOrSolid.
- Telegraph lanes require positive lane count, width, and active ticks; authored arena-collision extension; no projectile speed/radius; and Lane/LeaveTelegraph/ExpireAtAuthoredEnd grammar.
- Fixed timelines require a positive loop, nonempty events, stable referenced pattern IDs, offsets strictly inside the loop, and deterministic strict ordering by `(offset, pattern_id, action)`.
- Require nonzero raw damage, lifetime, threat, and declared maximum active instances. Validate maximum active instances against exact context cap: normal `300`; boss/pinnacle `500`.
- Require the shared normalized defaults unless explicitly expanded by a later record: zero acceleration, no player pierce, and `cancel_on_phase_change=true`.
- Require a fairness fixture at exactly player speed `4.5 tiles/s`, hurtbox radius `0.25`, RTT `120 ms`, and no movement ability. Normal and benchmark-boss safe corridors must be at least `0.80 tiles`; pinnacle may use `0.65`.
- Measure corridor geometry edge-to-edge after projectile/hazard radii. Baseline player-center clearance is exactly `0.40 tiles` per boundary: radius `0.25` plus clearance `0.15`.
- Reject a hostile projectile that can reach the current player position in less than `350 ms`, except a visibly repeating beam with an inactive warning phase. Use integer ceiling travel time; do not use float tolerance.
- When hostile origin distance is below `1.25 tiles`, require at least `750 ms` of existing ground telegraph.
- Every pattern declares at least one compatibility tag. Reject any pair where one pattern's tag intersects the other's forbidden tags.
- When Frostbind may overlap another mandatory pattern, require an exact `4.0 tiles/s` compatibility fixture; otherwise the combination must fail closed as unsafe/forbidden.
- Accumulate typed validation diagnostics in deterministic sorted/deduplicated order so content tooling can report all errors in one pass.
- Present authoritative timeline cues, warning/resolve/active intervals, current and maximum threat/projectile counts, compatibility/Frostbind failures, and corridor geometry. Presentation cannot decide validation or pattern timing.

## Legal transitions

- Definition: `Unvalidated -> Validated(definition, warning_ticks) | Rejected(sorted diagnostics)`.
- Timeline: `LoopStart -> Telegraph/Preview -> Resolve -> ActiveEnd -> LoopRestart`; equal-offset events use strict stable tuple order.
- Phase change: `Scheduled/Active -> Cancelled` when the owning phase policy is `cancel_on_phase_change=true`.
- Combination: `IndividuallyValid -> Compatible | Rejected(tag conflict | Frostbind unsafe)`.
- Debug presentation: `Authoritative snapshot/events -> Overlay`; overlay state never feeds simulation.

## Failure behavior

- Invalid ID/cue derivation, missing Major+ audio, short warning, zero raw/lifetime/threat/cap, invalid kind grammar, missing projectile geometry, invalid timeline, wrong baseline fixture, narrow corridor, fast arrival, unsafe close spawn, empty/conflicting compatibility, unsupported player pierce/acceleration, or missing phase cancellation yields typed rejection.
- A definition with multiple defects returns every stable diagnostic; iteration order cannot change output.
- Adapters copy scheduler-owned values. They cannot silently substitute pattern defaults, change damage/band, or infer different geometry.
- A declared fairness fixture is not sufficient proof until the solver validates the actual arena/path. Missing solver evidence keeps the milestone pending.
- Client/debug errors cannot change content validity, schedule, threat, active count, compatibility, or phase cancellation.
