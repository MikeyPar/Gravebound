# GB-M01-02B — Projectile and hitbox collision

**Status:** Approved implementation contract.

## Task contract

**Task ID:** `GB-M01-02B`  
**Title:** Projectile and hitbox collision  
**Milestone:** M01 — First Playable  
**Feature IDs:** `GB-M01-02B`, `COM-001`, `CLS-020`, `CONT-FP-002`

**Player-visible outcome:** Pine Crossbow bolts stop exactly where their circular hitbox first touches the Bell Laboratory shell, a pillar, or an enemy hurtbox. Every contact has readable debug geometry and distinct blocked-versus-enemy feedback, while health remains unchanged until `GB-M01-05A`.

**In scope:**

- Simulation-owned immutable solid geometry compiled from `arena.prototype.bell_laboratory_01`.
- Simulation-owned circular enemy hurtbox records with stable entity IDs.
- Continuous swept-circle collision against the arena shell, pillar rectangles, and enemy circles.
- Deterministic earliest-contact selection, exact terminal positions, zero-pierce termination, and one collision event per terminated projectile.
- Static LocalLab debug targets, projectile/hurtbox outlines, and distinct enemy-hit and solid-block presentation.
- Unit, deterministic, integration, adversarial, visual, optimized-runtime, and CI verification.

**Out of scope:**

- Health, armor, resistance, damage order, death, damage numbers, enemy AI, rewards, drops, or hostile projectiles. `GB-M01-05A` owns damage mutation.
- Pierce continuation and per-target ignore sets beyond preserving the authored pierce field for later ability/item tickets.
- Grave Mark, Slipstep, Stillness, production enemy spawning, production sprites, audio, networking, and persistence.
- Moving enemy hurtboxes during a tick. The future authoritative world must provide the tick-start hurtbox snapshot consumed by collision.

**Authoritative owner:** `sim_core::collision` owns collider types, validation, sweep math, target ordering, and collision events; `sim_core::combat` owns projectile lifecycle and event emission.  
**Client presentation owner:** `client_bevy::combat` mirrors simulation events and immutable debug hurtboxes; Bevy entities and transforms never decide a hit.

**Dependencies:**

- Code modules: `sim_core::arena`, `sim_core::collision`, `sim_core::combat`, `sim_content`, `client_bevy::combat`, `client_bevy::arena_view`.
- Content IDs/schemas: `arena.prototype.bell_laboratory_01`, `item.prototype.weapon.pine_crossbow`, `fp.1.0.0`.
- Previous tasks: `GB-M01-01A`, `GB-M01-01B`, `GB-M01-02A`.

**Exact rules and prototype defaults:**

- One simulation tile is one world unit; authored northwest coordinates and the fixed 30 Hz clock remain authoritative.
- Pine Crossbow projectiles use radius `0.10`, speed `12 tiles/s`, range `9.5`, one bolt, zero pierce, and stop on the first enemy.
- A full travel tick sweeps at most `0.4 tiles`; range clamps the candidate segment before collision testing.
- Solids are the four boundaries of the exact `32 × 24` walkable region and the four sorted `CONT-FP-002` pillar rectangles. Pillar index is its canonical `sim_content` sort order.
- Solid stable IDs are `shell.north`, `shell.south`, `shell.west`, `shell.east`, and `pillar.<zero-based canonical index>`.
- Enemy hurtboxes are closed circles with a unique nonzero `EntityId`, finite center, and finite positive radius. Their effective sweep radius is `projectile.radius + enemy.radius`.
- Tangency is a collision. A projectile beginning in contact/overlap reports travel fraction `0` and may not escape through the collider.
- Pillar collision is the exact Minkowski sum of the rectangle and projectile circle: face slabs plus rounded corner arcs. A plain expanded AABB false-positive at a rounded corner is prohibited.
- Candidate comparison is travel fraction using `f32::total_cmp`. The numerically earliest contact wins. Equal fractions resolve solid before enemy; solid ties use stable solid ID; enemy ties use `EntityId`.
- The projectile center moves to `start + displacement × fraction`. Its cumulative distance increases only by the realized travel distance. The event stores that exact center and distance.
- A zero-pierce Pine Crossbow bolt terminates on the winning contact and emits exactly one `ProjectileCollision`; it cannot also expire at range or contact a later target in that tick.
- Collision processing iterates projectiles in ascending projectile ID and collision events preserve that order.
- Static LocalLab debug targets are nondamageable and exist only to prove circular enemy contact before production enemy state arrives.

**Legal state transitions:**

- Projectile: `Spawned -> Travelling -> CollidedSolid | CollidedEnemy | ExpiredAtRange`.
- A projectile has exactly one terminal transition and is absent from active state after that tick.
- Collision world: `Construct -> Validate -> ImmutableForTick`; invalid construction never enters simulation.
- Client presentation: `ActiveBolt -> ContactEffect -> Despawned`; presentation may lag but cannot alter the terminal result.

**Rejected inputs and typed errors:**

- Empty/invalid arena geometry, non-finite or nonpositive projectile radius, non-finite segment endpoints, non-finite enemy center/radius, zero radius, duplicate enemy IDs, or an enemy hurtbox overlapping a solid at world construction fail closed.
- Non-finite calculated contact fractions or terminal positions fail the entire combat step transactionally.
- Unknown/missing presentation entities do not change simulation; they log or recover presentation without inventing a contact.

**Network messages/version behavior:** none in M01. Future snapshots must preserve projectile ID, tick, collision target ID, terminal position, and cumulative distance. The server remains final under `SIM-010`/`SIM-011`.

**Persistence and idempotency:** none. Collision events are ephemeral and uniquely identified within a run by `(tick, projectile_id)` because a projectile can terminate once.

**UI states:**

- Loading: no window opens before content, arena, weapon, and collision-world validation succeeds.
- Empty: no active bolts; all static target and solid debug outlines remain visible.
- Success: enemy contact shows a readable bone-white/teal impact; solid contact shows an amber blocked mark.
- Disabled: not applicable; collision cannot be disabled in LocalLab.
- Error: invalid authoritative state fails loudly with feature and collider context.
- Disconnected: not applicable before M02.
- Retry: restart LocalLab; no collision state persists.

**Telemetry events/fields:** no product telemetry before `GB-M01-10B`. Debug logs include feature ID, tick, projectile ID, collision target stable ID/kind, terminal position, and travelled distance.

**Accessibility/readability:** collision and hurtbox debug states use shape plus luminance, not color alone. Enemy circles, solid expansion outlines, bolt circles, and contact marks remain legible in grayscale. Friendly projectiles retain bone-white priority below future hostile projectiles, and persistent diagnostics stay outside the center aiming corridor.

**Tests required:**

- Unit: exact shell, pillar face, rounded-corner, and enemy-circle sweeps; tangent, overlap, no-hit, range-clamped segment, and terminal position/distance.
- Deterministic: repeated fixed collision traces pin projectile IDs, ticks, targets, float-bit terminal positions, distances, and event order.
- Integration: validated `fp.1.0.0` geometry produces the exact stable solid set; LocalLab consumes the same immutable collision world it presents.
- Adversarial/failure: 12-tiles/s no-tunneling shots, corner near-miss versus corner hit, tangent from both directions, start overlap, shell/pillar/enemy equal-contact ties, duplicate/reordered enemies, non-finite inputs, and transactional failure.
- Content validation: unchanged strict `CONT-FP-002` and Pine Crossbow compilation fixtures pass.
- Visual/manual: optimized LocalLab evidence visibly proves projectile circles, hurtbox circles, solid boundaries, enemy hit, solid block, and updated collision diagnostics.

**Feature flag/kill switch:** none for LocalLab.  
**Rollback/migration behavior:** revert feature commits. Any change to sweep math, tie precedence, terminal event shape, collider IDs, or collision tick order requires an ADR revision and deterministic fixture migration.

## Acceptance criteria

1. The `0.10`-tile Pine Crossbow circle cannot tunnel through the exact shell, any `CONT-FP-002` pillar, or a simulation-owned enemy hurtbox at `12 tiles/s`, including corner, tangent, and initial-overlap cases.
2. Every projectile resolves at most one terminal state per tick; earliest contact and exact-tie precedence are deterministic, stable-ID ordered, and produce exact terminal center/distance values.
3. Zero-pierce enemy or solid contact removes the projectile and emits one typed collision event without health, damage, death, reward, or inventory mutation.
4. LocalLab visibly renders projectile and target hitboxes plus distinct enemy-hit/solid-block feedback while presentation cannot decide or alter collision.
5. Formatting, warnings-as-errors Clippy, workspace tests, strict content validation, deterministic traces, optimized Windows runtime, and clean GitHub CI pass.
