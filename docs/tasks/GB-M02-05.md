# GB-M02-05 — Deterministic adverse-network harness

## Objective

Drive the real protocol, authority session, prediction/reconciliation runtime, and connection lifecycle through reproducible adverse delivery without wall-clock sleeps or a second gameplay model.

## Authorities

1. `Gravebound_Production_GDD_v1_Canonical.md`: `SIM-012`, `TECH-011`–`TECH-015`, `QA-005`, `QA-006`, and the M02 exit gate.
2. `Gravebound_Content_Production_Spec_v1.md`: immutable `fp.1.0.0` remains the sole combat content source; the harness cannot replace authored gameplay with synthetic balance values.
3. `Gravebound_Development_Roadmap_v1.md`: `GB-M02-05` and the 100 ms RTT / 20 ms jitter / 1% loss playability and authoritative-death requirement.
4. Repository `ADR-022-deterministic-transport-impairment.md`.

## Required implementation

- Add a socket-free `network_harness` crate that schedules actual protocol-encoded frames on an explicit monotonic microsecond clock.
- Support independent direction, base RTT, symmetric jitter, datagram loss, datagram duplication, datagram reordering, and bounded outage windows.
- Pin all QA-006 profiles: 20/0/0/0 baseline; 80/10/0.5%/0.1%; 120/20/1%/0.5%; 180/40/2%/1%; 250/80/5%/2%; and 0.5–5 second outages. Also pin the roadmap M02 exit profile at 100 ms RTT, 20 ms jitter, and 1% loss.
- Apply loss/duplication/reordering only to datagram messages. Reliable Control/Action/Mutation/Pattern/Social messages retain exactly-once ordered application delivery outside explicit connection loss.
- Encode every submitted `WireMessage`, queue only bounded bytes, decode on delivery, and retain transport/channel validation. No direct Rust-object shortcut may bypass the codec.
- Use a named deterministic seed and stable draw order. Identical profiles, seeds, messages, and clock advances must produce byte-identical deliveries and diagnostics.
- Emit explicit link-down/link-up transitions for outage orchestration. The harness reports transport facts; `SessionDirectory` remains final for LinkLost, Recall, reconnect, and death.
- Record submitted, delivered, lost, outage-dropped, duplicated, reordered, queued, and maximum-queue metrics without account/auth data.
- Drive a scripted `fp.1.0.0` combat session at 30 Hz through the 100/20/1 profile. Feed delivered inputs only through `ManagedSession`, snapshots only through `RemoteClientRuntime`, and preserve authoritative death finality.
- Emit an immediate critical snapshot on an authoritative death tick even when it falls between baseline 15 Hz snapshot ticks.

## Bounds

- Basis points are `0..=10000`.
- At most `8` nonoverlapping outage windows, each `500 ms..=5 s`.
- At most `65,536` queued encoded frames and `64 MiB` queued bytes.
- Clock movement is monotonic; all time arithmetic is checked.
- Reliable per-channel delivery order is strict within each direction.

## Explicitly deferred

- Malicious semantic input/mutation rejection beyond transport duplication/staleness: `GB-M02-06`.
- Complete automated journey and two-hour bot soak: `GB-M02-07`.
- Multi-instance scheduler and production tick percentiles: `GB-M02-08`.
- Human four-player impairment approval remains part of the full M02 gate.

## Acceptance

- Golden tests pin all profiles, deterministic replay, codec traversal, queue bounds, monotonic time, directionality, reliable ordering, datagram loss/duplication/reordering, and outage transitions.
- At the 100/20/1 roadmap profile, scripted movement remains responsive, correction snaps stay within the `SIM-012` ordinary-sample allowance, and final client/server movement converges after drain.
- If the authoritative player dies, the delivered client death result matches the authoritative tick/state and cannot be predicted or suppressed by loss.
- Outage tests drive LinkLost and prove both pre-deadline reconnect and 90-tick Recall resolution through the existing lifecycle owner.
- Networking CI and full workspace CI pass with format, warnings-denied Clippy, content validation, and identical deterministic traces before README advances.
