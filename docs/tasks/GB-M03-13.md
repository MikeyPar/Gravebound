## Eligibility, snapshot, and promotion invariants

- Eligibility is evaluated from locked authoritative death/life records only: level `>= 10`; `permadeath_combat_ticks >= 18_000`; at least one reward-qualified dungeon boss or two distinct reward-qualified major realm events; and no verified server-incident or administrative death.
- A death can produce at most one Echo, uniquely keyed by `death_id`. Ineligible death returns a typed stored `NotCreated(reason)` projection without inserting an Echo; exact retry returns the same reason.
- Build `EchoRecord` from the committed death snapshot: echo/death/account IDs, approved character-name snapshot, class, optional Oath, level, appearance snapshot/theme, weapon/relic signature presentation tags, ordered active Bargain IDs, deed tags, killer content/pattern, death region, power band, created time, state, and content revision.
- Never copy an item UID, item roll, affix roll, purchased cosmetic entitlement, mutable live pointer, or localized string. Missing/disabled/incompatible content or malformed snapshot aborts the whole death transaction.
- Insert eligible Echo as `Dormant` and append the immutable creation transition. Under the same account lock, if no Echo is `Available`, promote the oldest Dormant by `(created_at, echo_id)` and append `Dormant -> Available`. The newly inserted Echo may therefore commit as Available.
- Enforce at most one Available Echo per account with a database constraint plus aggregate locking. Concurrent eligible deaths for one account serialize and produce deterministic queue order without duplicate promotion.
- Projector failure, uniqueness conflict, invalid eligibility evidence, transition-audit failure, or outbox failure aborts death, destruction, summary, and memorial writes. Publish outbox/telemetry records only as committed transaction rows; external delivery is post-commit.
- Existing Available/Defeated/Archived/Disabled states are not otherwise changed by M03 death projection. Practice death, server-fault restoration, and administrative action never create an Echo.
