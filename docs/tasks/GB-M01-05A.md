## Required behavior

- Represent the First Playable direct damage types as closed simulation enums: `Physical` and `Veil`. Do not add critical-hit, random-evasion, cheat-death, or resurrection inputs or outputs.
- Create a direct-hit request only after validating distinct existing source/target IDs, authoritative collision, target immunity, positive raw damage, legal resolved stats, health state, optional cap, and the attack's authored type/band provenance. Invalid or immune contacts never enter health resolution.
- Enforce the `100 ms` global hostile-projectile grace as exactly 3 fixed ticks. A successful hostile projectile hit starts grace. A nonpiercing projectile colliding during grace is consumed and deals zero. A piercing projectile records the player as grace-ignored, continues, and can never damage that player later. Ground hazards and damage-over-time do not consume this projectile grace.
- Resolve a legal direct hit in exact `COM-002` order using checked integer fixed point at `1/10000`: raw damage; attacker multiplier; resistance; strongest valid direct reduction; armor; one half-up whole-damage conversion with minimum 1 while positive; barrier; optional declared health cap; health.
- Clamp ordinary resistance symmetrically to `-2,500..=2,500 basis points`. Negative resistance increases damage. Preserve both input and applied values in the event.
- Select only the strongest valid directional/self/ally direct-damage reduction. Do not stack reductions. A legal 100% reduction may reduce the fixed-point hit to zero before armor.
- Compute `armor_reduction = min(armor, damage_after_reduction * 0.35)`. Carry armor and fractional damage at fixed precision. Armor cannot erase more than 35% of that stage.
- Round only once to whole damage, immediately after armor, using half-up. Preserve zero when prior legal reduction made the hit zero; otherwise enforce minimum 1.
- Absorb `min(current_barrier, post_armor_damage)` before a health cap. Update barrier by exactly the absorbed amount. Fully absorbed hits deal zero health damage.
- Ordinary attacks declare no health cap. When an explicitly validated future record supplies a max-health-share cap, calculate a conservative integer cap that never exceeds its declared percentage, record the reduction, and apply it after barrier.
- Apply remaining damage saturating at current health. Record potential post-cap health damage separately from actual health lost to preserve lethal overkill information. Return exact new barrier and health values and a `lethal` flag.
- Commit the `DamageEvent`, new barrier, and shared `PlayerVitals` atomically. Invoke `break_focused_from_damage` for received damage before any later same-tick primary action. A lethal result rejects later character actions and enters the `GB-M01-06A` death handoff in that same tick.
- Emit one `DamageEvent` containing source, target, type, raw damage, all multipliers/clamps/fixed intermediates, armor limit/reduction, rounded damage, barrier values, cap values, health before/potential/applied/after, resolved band, and lethal flag.
- Classify final health damage against max health as Chip through Execution with exact boundaries: Chip up to 8%; Pressure above 8% through 18%; Major above 18% through 35%; Severe above 35% through 60%; Execution above 60%. Runtime zero damage has no band. Strict authored hostile validation rejects positive reference damage below 1%, a declared/resolved mismatch, and Execution when the stage does not explicitly permit it.
- Validate M01 attacks against the resolved reference Grave Arbalist loadout, including starting health/armor and equipped Reedcloth health. Standard First Playable attacks must not validate as Execution or exceed 60% of legal reference max health.
- Present current/max health, barrier, final damage number/direction, damage band/readability treatment, projectile-grace ignore diagnostics, and complete debug intermediates without obscuring the aiming corridor. Hostile feedback must remain shape/luminance-readable without color alone.

## Legal transitions

- Contact: `Collision -> ValidatedDirectHit | Immune | ProjectileGraceIgnored`; invalid state fails without mutation.
- Grace: `Ready -> HitApplied -> Grace(3) -> Grace(2) -> Grace(1) -> Ready`; ground/DoT paths do not enter this state.
- Direct hit: `Raw -> AttackerScaled -> ResistanceApplied -> StrongestReductionApplied -> ArmorApplied -> Rounded -> BarrierAbsorbed -> OptionalCapApplied -> HealthApplied`.
- Barrier: `Barrier(n) -> Barrier(n - absorbed)` where `absorbed=min(n, post_armor)`.
- Health: `Alive(h) -> Alive(h-d)` or `Alive(h) -> Lethal(0)`; a target already at zero cannot accept another direct-hit request.
- Focused: `Focused + received damage -> BrokenByDamage` before later same-tick fire.
- Lethal handoff: `HealthApplied(0) -> ActionsRejected -> DeathPending(GB-M01-06A)` in the same authoritative tick.

## Failure behavior

- Same source/target, missing collision, immunity, zero raw damage, zero attacker multiplier, reduction above 100%, invalid cap, zero/invalid health, dead target, unknown damage type, missing band provenance, arithmetic overflow, or inconsistent commit state fails closed.
- The direct-hit transaction must stage resolver output before mutating barrier, health, grace, Focused, hostile projectile disposition, or lethal state. All fields commit or none commit.
- A projectile-grace ignored contact cannot accidentally start a damage request. Piercing target-ignore state and debug event commit together.
- Presentation errors cannot change health, barrier, grace, bands, projectile consumption, Focused, or lethal state.
- A mismatch between authored and resolved reference damage band fails content validation; it cannot be downgraded to a warning.
