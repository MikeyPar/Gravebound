## Integrated lifecycle contract

- Use production framing, validation, authentication, selected-character binding, service coordinators, and PostgreSQL repositories; the fixture may seed approved disposable preconditions but may not call a coordinator in place of a lifecycle action that has a protocol surface.
- Compose character creation/selection, starter backfill, level/XP projection, durable reward placement, four-slot equipment, CharacterSafe/Vault transfers, explicit deliberate risk, and dormant danger-entry storage preflight.
- Every item mutation remains content-bound, source/version-bound, server-planned, idempotent, and atomic. The client never supplies a destination, security state, item version, resolved stat, placement map, or authoritative result.
- Reconnect and process restart reconstruct the exact selected character, level/XP, starter/reward identities and provenance, equipment, Belt/backpack/CharacterSafe/Vault projections, item/aggregate versions, and replay receipts.
- The harness advertises a disposable integration feature only. Normal Character Select `Play`, Realm Gate and Vault station interaction, Core promotion, and the normal player route remain disabled.

## Required evidence

- One legal real-QUIC/PostgreSQL lifecycle crosses every completed `04A`-`04F` mutation family and reconstructs an exact canonical signature before reconnect, after reconnect, and after a newly bound server process starts on the same database.
- Dropped-response exact retries return the stored outcome and never duplicate XP, first-clear credit, reward UIDs, starter rows, item ledgers, equipment changes, safe-storage placements, or aggregate versions.
- Changed-payload reuse, stale versions, foreign account/character/item bindings, invalid content revisions, malformed/oversized frames, caller-authored authority, unavailable PostgreSQL, and corrupt durable state produce typed fail-closed results with no partial mutation.
- Disconnects at each mutation boundary, duplicate sessions, concurrent same-item claims, final-capacity races, serialization/deadlock victims, and injected repository/provider failures converge to one committed result or none.
- Normal-route negatives prove zero production admission, instance allocation, location mutation, extraction conversion, death, successor, Overflow/ResolutionHold, station enablement, or Core promotion.
- A deterministic multi-journey fixture records login-to-control and mutation round-trip median/p95/max, confirms every value remains inside the existing M03 limits, and leaves no sessions, tasks, or database locks behind.
- Optimized Windows construction and a compact native inspection surface prove the complete item/Vault state remains legible at 1280x720 and 1920x1080 in standard and reduced-effects modes without obscuring the playfield or inventing production station interaction.
