# GB-M01-01B — Input map and player movement

**Status:** In progress.

## Task contract

**Task ID:** `GB-M01-01B`  
**Title:** Input map and player movement  
**Milestone:** M01 — First Playable  
**Feature IDs:** `GB-M01-01B`

**Player-visible outcome:** LocalLab starts a controllable level-1 Grave Arbalist at `(4,12)`. WASD moves at the authored final speed, diagonal movement has no speed advantage, solid shell and pillars block the player, and the orthographic camera follows smoothly without influencing gameplay state.

**In scope:**

- A renderer-independent, fixed-30-Hz player movement state in `sim_core`.
- Rebind-ready `move` action bindings with WASD defaults.
- Per-render-frame keyboard sampling and latest-state consumption at the next fixed tick.
- Normalized cardinal/diagonal input, two-tick direction response, and `5.1 tiles/second` final speed.
- Circle-versus-arena collision using a `0.30 tile` player physical radius.
- A visible player avatar, live movement diagnostics, and an `80 ms` critically damped presentation camera.
- Unit, deterministic, integration, adversarial, visual, and release verification.

**Out of scope:**

- Controller input, settings persistence, and a key-rebinding menu. The binding data model must support those additions without changing simulation code.
- Aim, weapons, projectiles, enemy bodies, player-player collision, camera shake, user zoom controls, animation, audio, networking, and persistence.

**Authoritative owner:** `sim_core::movement`; Bevy keyboard state, transforms, and camera state are never authoritative.  
**Client presentation owner:** `client_bevy::player` and `client_bevy::arena_view`.

**Dependencies:**

- Code modules: `sim_core`, `sim_content`, `client_bevy`.
- Content IDs/schemas: `class.grave_arbalist`, `arena.prototype.bell_laboratory_01`, `fp.1.0.0`.
- Previous task: `GB-M01-01A`.

**Exact rules and prototype defaults:**

- `SIM-001`: movement uses tiles/simulation units and northwest-authored `+x east, +y south` coordinates.
- `SIM-002`: camera follow is a presentation-only critically damped spring with an `80 ms` response constant.
- `SIM-003`: `move` defaults to WASD, diagonals normalize, and bindings are replaceable data rather than system constants.
- `SIM-004`: input is sampled each rendered frame; the latest complete movement action is consumed by a fixed `30 Hz` simulation tick.
- `SIM-005`: direction acceleration/deceleration is `60 ms`, final speed is unaffected, physical radius is `0.30 tiles`, and authored solids block the player.
- `CLS-020`: the Grave Arbalist final speed is exactly `5.1 tiles/second`.
- `CONT-010`: ordinary `60 ms` rounds to `2` ticks at 30 Hz; coordinates use `+x east, +y south`.
- `CONT-FP-002`: spawn, shell, bounds, and pillar rectangles come from the compiled arena record.
- Digital input axes are integers in `[-1,1]`. The vector is normalized when both axes are nonzero. Opposing keys cancel on their axis.
- Each tick, velocity moves toward `normalized_input × 5.1` by at most `5.1 / 2 = 2.55 tiles/second`. Rest-to-full and full-to-rest therefore take two ticks; a full reversal takes four ticks because its velocity delta is twice as large. Once settled, response smoothing cannot reduce final speed.
- Tick displacement is `velocity / 30`. Collision is evaluated in bounded substeps no longer than half the physical radius, followed by deterministic circle-versus-rectangle depenetration in authored pillar order. Inward velocity is removed at a collision normal so tangential motion slides.
- The walkable center bounds are `[0.30,width-0.30] × [0.30,height-0.30]`. Touching a solid boundary is legal; penetration is not.
- The camera applies an analytic critically damped spring after simulation. It reads the player position and writes only the camera transform and camera spring velocity.

**Legal state transitions:** `Boot -> ValidateContent -> SpawnPlayer -> Running`; while running, each fixed tick performs `SampledAction -> TargetVelocity -> SmoothedVelocity -> CollisionResolvedPosition -> PresentedTransform`. Window close transitions `Running -> Stopped`.

**Rejected inputs and typed errors:** non-finite position/velocity, position outside walkable bounds, spawn intersecting a pillar, invalid movement constants, and invalid arena geometry fail construction. Runtime digital axes are clamped to `[-1,1]`; conflicting bindings cancel rather than choose by event order.

**Network messages/version behavior:** none. The movement action/state boundary is intentionally compact and renderer-independent for the later 30 Hz client/server message.

**Persistence and idempotency:** none. Binding persistence is deferred; default bindings are recreated on each launch.

**UI states:**

- Loading: no interactive frame before content validation and movement-state construction.
- Success: controllable avatar plus WASD, speed, position, and camera diagnostics.
- Disabled: movement intent is neutral when focus/input is unavailable.
- Error: typed startup error before the Bevy app runs.
- Empty/Disconnected/Retry: not applicable in local M01.

**Telemetry events/fields:** no product telemetry before `GB-M01-10B`; startup log includes feature ID, spawn, speed, collision radius, and response ticks.

**Accessibility/readability:** the avatar has a high-luminance silhouette distinct from hostile red; the HUD identifies controls in text; movement does not depend on color, camera shake, or animation.

**Tests required:**

- Unit: opposing-key cancellation, cardinal/diagonal normalization, two-tick acceleration/deceleration, final 5.1 speed, fixed-tick travel, coordinate conversion, and camera spring convergence.
- Deterministic: identical input sequences produce bit-identical movement snapshots; cardinal and diagonal paths have equal length within floating-point tolerance over equal steady-state tick counts.
- Integration: compiled First Playable arena creates the player at `(4,12)` and the Bevy binding resource defaults to WASD.
- Adversarial/failure: sustained movement cannot penetrate any shell edge or pillar; diagonal corner pressure remains finite and nonpenetrating; invalid construction fails closed.
- Visual/manual: release client visibly moves, slides at solids, follows without camera overshoot, and remains responsive; capture a representative frame.
- Regression: camera update cannot mutate the authoritative movement resource.

**Feature flag/kill switch:** none; LocalLab is the only M01 runtime surface.  
**Rollback/migration behavior:** revert the feature commits. Changing speed, response ticks, radius, or collision policy requires a GDD/content review, this contract update, and regenerated movement fixtures.

## Acceptance criteria

1. Rebind-ready WASD actions drive a simulation-owned Grave Arbalist at a settled `5.1 tiles/second`; cardinal and diagonal travel match over equal fixed tick counts without diagonal gain.
2. The `0.30 tile` physical circle never enters the compiled arena shell or any of its four pillar rectangles, including sustained diagonal corner pressure.
3. The camera follows with an `80 ms` critically damped presentation spring and no camera code can change authoritative position or velocity.
4. Unit, deterministic, content, adversarial, visual, full local CI, and Windows release checks pass.
