## Presentation contract and invariants

- The client enters death-summary state only from a validated `06A` result that names a durably committed death. Local health zero, disconnect, timeout, or predicted terminality may show a nonfinal waiting state but never the summary.
- Render stored `DTH-020` data in this exact order: hero/class/level/lifetime/final deed; killer/attack/damage/type/source position; last-five timeline; network state; `Lost`; `Preserved`; `Created`; primary `Create successor`; secondary trace, memorial, and character-select actions.
- The summary consumes stable IDs/numbers/content revision and resolves copy through the matching compiled content/localization package. Missing content, invalid revision, corrupt ordering, or unknown discriminant fails closed to a recoverable support-safe error; it never guesses copy.
- `Lost`, `Preserved`, and `Created` are the committed snapshot projections. They are not recomputed from current inventory, Vault, Echo, or account state after the death.
- `Create successor` is the largest action and delegates only to `GB-M03-07`. Store, paid-product, and promotional surfaces are absent until the successor returns to control.
- The Memorial Wall is read-only, account-bound, paginated, and ordered newest-first by `(death_at DESC, death_id)`. Selection opens the exact stored summary revision; it performs no gameplay mutation and cannot view another account.
- The Core station follows the existing responsive Hall presentation rules, retains a protected playfield corridor, uses non-color state cues, supports keyboard/controller/pointer focus, meets effective 14 px text minimums, and preserves information in reduced-effects mode.
- Summary interactive target is within two seconds of durable acknowledgement. Performance evidence separately records death-to-summary and Memorial list/detail latency without counting database-unavailable waiting as a committed death.
